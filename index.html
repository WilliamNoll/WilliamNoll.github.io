Welcome to Will Noll's Documentation of Attempting to Change the World

William Noll

I’m a PhD student in Computational Neuroscience at Carnegie Mellon University, advised by Drs. Byron Yu, Steve Chase, and Aaron Batista. I'm interested in brain-computer interfaces (BCIs), designing machine learning tools to analyze neural popuation activity, and neural engineering. My PhD work aims to leverage BCIs as a research tool to learn more how the brain controls movement. In the longterm, I plan to use BCIs for clinical applications to help people with tetraplegia, spinal cord injury, and blindness.

I am always excited on working on impactful projects. Send me an email if you're interested in collaboration!
